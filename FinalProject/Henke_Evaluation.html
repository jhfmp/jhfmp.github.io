Summary of Evaluators' Comments

Overall, the evaluators liked my classroom website. A common bit of feedback I was to look into the greens that I selected for my color scheme. A fellow classmate and evaluator 1 both stated this could be an issue. Evaluator 2 said the color scheme was consistent, but possibly bland. Since some users weren't too keen on the greens, and another user indentified the scheme as bland it will be an area to investigate possible alternatives.

Additionally, many of the evalutors pointed out that the final two pages of the website need to be completed. I still need to complete the Useful Documents page, and the Educational Games page. This will provide a more complete and better end user expereince once these have been completed.

When I observed evaluator 2 navigate the website, we noticed there are some issues with the positioning of the navigational links on the top of Important Documents and Educational Games. They do not match up with the rest of the pages on the website. In order to ensure consistency, I will need to correct this issue. Evaluator 2 atttempted to help me figure out why the code in these 2 pages was creating a slightly different expereince than the rest of the pages, but we were unable to determine where the mistake was made. This is something I work on sorting out for the final version of my website.

Reflections on Evaluation Process:

Evaluations are always a useful way to find out more about something one has created. It is difficult to know if something that makes sense to one person will make sense to others. The only real way to know for sure is to get feedback from other people. That is exactly the reason why receiving feedback on our websites is so valuable. I do not know if the layout of my site is good unless I ask another person their opinion on it.

After asking several people to look at my website, I received feedback that will help me improve the site to meet all of its intended purposes. I know I need to complete 2 pages that will allow me to satisfy 2 of my Use Cases. One being a parent who needs to get a useful document that might have been lost when it was sent home. The Use Case is a student who needs to practices skills at home with an education game that is linked on my site.

Additionally, I know there may be some improvements that can be made with my color combination choices. Also, some spelling mistakes need to be corrected, and finally there is some coding that can be sorted out to improve consistency of the website. Without the evaluation process I might not have noticed these things on my own. By using the process I now know of these issues and work to fix them. This will help me create an even better classroom website than I could have created on my own.
